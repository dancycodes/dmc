## What It Does
Renders the hero section at the top of the cook's tenant landing page. Displays the cook's brand name prominently, a tagline or short bio excerpt, and a cover image carousel (up to 5 images) with auto-animation. Includes a call-to-action button ("View Meals" or "Order Now") that scrolls to the meals grid section. Shows the cook's average star rating and total review count as a compact badge.

---

## Who Uses It
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Client (guest or authenticated) | Views the hero section to understand the cook's brand and click through to meals |
| Cook | Sees how their brand is presented in the hero section |

---

## User Scenarios
### Scenario 1: Full hero section
A visitor arrives at `latifa.dm.test`. The hero section shows "Chef Latifa" in large typography, "Authentic Cameroonian flavors, made with love" as the tagline, a carousel of 4 cover images cycling every 5 seconds with smooth transitions, a "View Meals" button, and a badge showing "4.7 stars (128 reviews)".

### Scenario 2: Cook with one image
A cook has only one cover image uploaded. The hero displays that single image as a static background without carousel animation. All other elements render normally.

### Scenario 3: Cook with no images
A cook has no cover images. The hero section displays a gradient background using the tenant's theme colors instead. The brand name, tagline, CTA button, and rating still display.

### Scenario 4: New cook with no reviews
A recently onboarded cook has zero reviews. Instead of showing "0 stars", the rating badge shows "New Cook" or is hidden entirely. The CTA button still appears.

### Scenario 5: Mobile view
On mobile, the hero section takes less vertical space. The cover images display at a smaller aspect ratio. The brand name, tagline, and CTA button stack vertically and are centered.

## Acceptance Criteria
- **Given** a cook with 3 cover images, **when** the hero loads, **then** the images auto-cycle every 5 seconds
- **Given** a cook with no cover images, **when** the hero loads, **then** a gradient background using theme colors is displayed
- **Given** a cook with 4.7 average rating and 128 reviews, **when** the hero loads, **then** "4.7" with stars and "(128 reviews)" is displayed
- **Given** a cook with zero reviews, **when** the hero loads, **then** "New Cook" badge is shown instead of rating
- **Given** the visitor clicks the CTA button, **when** the click fires, **then** the page scrolls to the meals grid section
- **Given** a mobile viewport, **when** the hero loads, **then** all elements stack vertically and are centered

---

## Verification Steps
1. Visit a tenant site and confirm hero section displays cook name, tagline, images, and rating
2. Watch the carousel and confirm images auto-cycle at approximately 5-second intervals
3. Hover over the carousel on desktop and confirm it pauses
4. Click the CTA button and confirm smooth scroll to meals section
5. View a cook with no images and confirm gradient fallback
6. View a cook with no reviews and confirm "New Cook" badge
7. Test on mobile and confirm responsive vertical stacking

---

## Edge Cases & Exceptions
| Condition | Expected Behavior |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Cook has exactly 1 cover image | Single image displayed statically, no carousel animation |
| Cook bio is empty | Tagline area hidden or shows a minimal default like the cook's name |
| Cover image fails to load (broken URL) | Fallback to gradient background for that slot |
| Very long cook name | Font size scales down or name wraps to second line |
| Rating is exactly 5.0 | All 5 stars filled, displays "5.0" |

---

## UI/UX Notes
- Hero section takes approximately 60-70vh on desktop, 40-50vh on mobile
- Brand name in large, bold typography respecting tenant font choice
- Tagline in lighter weight below the brand name
- Carousel uses smooth fade transitions between images
- Semi-transparent overlay on images to ensure text readability
- CTA button in a prominent color from the tenant theme
- Rating badge positioned below or beside the CTA button
- Carousel navigation dots visible at bottom of image area
